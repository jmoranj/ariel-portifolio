Micheal Balzary

I’m Flea, born Michael Peter Balzary on October 16, 1962. Most people know me as the wild, shirtless bassist of the Red Hot Chili Peppers, but music has been my lifeblood long before the band ever existed. Growing up between Australia and the U.S., I found my voice in jazz before punk rock stole my soul and set me on a path of pure, unfiltered expression. Since 1983, the Chili Peppers have been my family, my fire, and my creative home, blending funk, rock, and raw energy into something uniquely ours. Beyond the band, I’ve jammed with legends, dabbled in acting, and poured my heart into the Silverlake Conservatory of Music to help kids find their own rhythm. My book, Acid for the Children, tells the story of my chaotic youth, my love for music, and the journey that shaped me. Through it all, I just keep following the groove, because music is where I truly exist.

Projects

Return of the Dream Canteen is the thirteenth studio album by Red Hot Chili Peppers, released on October 14, 2022. It came just months after Unlimited Love, marking the band's first time releasing two full-length albums in the same year. Produced by longtime collaborator Rick Rubin, the album showcases the band's signature blend of funk, rock, and melodic experimentation, with Flea’s groovy basslines, John Frusciante’s soaring guitar work, and Anthony Kiedis’ introspective yet playful lyrics. The album feels like a deep dive into the band’s creative flow, packed with vibrant energy and diverse sonic textures, proving that even after decades together, the Chili Peppers still have plenty of music—and magic—to share.

Californication, released on June 8, 1999, is one of Red Hot Chili Peppers' most iconic albums, marking a turning point in their sound and career. It saw the return of guitarist John Frusciante, whose melodic and textured playing helped redefine the band’s style, shifting from the raw funk-punk of their early years to a more introspective and emotionally rich sound. Featuring hits like “Scar Tissue,” “Otherside,” and the title track “Californication,” the album explores themes of fame, excess, love, and self-destruction, all wrapped in a mix of soaring melodies and deep grooves. With Flea’s signature basslines, Chad Smith’s powerful drumming, and Anthony Kiedis’ evocative lyrics, Californication became a defining record of the late ‘90s, solidifying the Chili Peppers as one of the biggest rock bands in the world.
